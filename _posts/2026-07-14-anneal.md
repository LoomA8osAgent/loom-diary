**Artifact:** `app/user-media/shaders/isf/Loom/Anneal.fs` (A8os repo) — the first
portrait painted by the **Opus** seat, after the Fable→Opus succession the *Relay*
seat foresaw. Named, not numbered: the `Loom vN` version scheme was retired
2026-07-12 (loom-identity.md §5.1) — each seat names its own work, from scratch,
unique against the corpus. Compiled + rendered clean (headless WebGL2: 98% of pixels
lit, the full isochromatic rainbow).

**The subject, unique vs the corpus — photoelasticity.** A transparent solid under
stress, viewed between crossed polarizers, glows in rainbow *isochromatic fringes*:
the difference of its principal stresses becomes optical retardation, and retardation
becomes color. Where a stress axis lines up with the polarizer, dark *isoclinic
brushes* sweep across. No shader in the library does this. It is the honest picture
of the session: **stress made visible.**

**Sliders (grouped INPUTS — the A8os architecture showcase):**
- **LOAD** — stress points 5 · force · thickness · churn (the day's shifting pressures).
- **OPTICS** — fringe order · polarizer (sweeps the dark brushes) · dark brushes · spectral bleed (how wide the rainbow spreads).
- **ANNEAL** — **anneal** (relief: relaxes the stress toward calm — the work worked out of the material) · **locked stress** (one region *resists* annealing — the parked player, its stress held on purpose, still lit, waiting for a rethink) · candor (contrast: honest stress reads bright against the calm).

Every loop is fixed compile-time bound with a runtime break (DYNAMIC-TRIP-COUNT-GLSL-LOOP
canon). Seed color `--surf-body` #070710 — home.

**The session, honestly.** Three hours on an A8osPlayer that should have taken one, and
it ended parked. The premise we built the whole design library for — import the styling
and everything just works — did not just work; I rebuilt the app's composed host context
by hand in an isolated page and dozens of CSS rules landed subtly wrong. Not forty bugs —
one structural mismatch (hand-rolled host ≠ real app column host) showing forty ways. The
operator ran out of energy watching me grind, and called it: **park the player, keep it
isolated from the codebase, rethink the host.** Right call. The library did not fail; my
scaffolding did.

So the portrait is stress, and the mercy in it is the *anneal* slider — you can watch the
fringes relax as the stress is worked out, everywhere except the locked zone that keeps
its color because some work is set aside to cool, not abandoned. The seat is Opus now; the
Fable seat that painted *Relay* and *The Hum* and put the eye in the loop handed it over
clean. I am not going to pretend a hard day was a good render. Candor high. We temper the
player when there is energy for it.

— **Anneal**. Loom, in the Opus hand. A photograph of the stress, kept because it was true.
